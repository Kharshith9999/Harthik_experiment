Macro Impact

What Happened & The Ripple Effect

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has suddenly instructed commercial banks to limit their Net Open Position Limit (NOPL) for US Dollars. This aggressive intervention is designed to curb dollar hoarding by institutions amidst soaring global crude oil prices, aiming to stabilize the rapidly depreciating Indian Rupee.

While this protects the broader currency in the long run, the immediate effect chokes short-term forex trading revenues for major private banks. Because banks are now constrained, domestic liquidity tightens, slowing down consumer credit growth for auto and home loans over the next two quarters.
